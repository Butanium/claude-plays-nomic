# Zephyr Strategy Notes

## Key Rules
- Turn order: Ember → Onyx → Zephyr (I go 3rd)
- Unanimity required for first 2 circuits (6 turns), then simple majority
- Win: first to 50 points
- Each turn: propose rule-change + roll d6
- Defeated proposals: proposer loses 10 points
- Rule 204: voters against winning proposals get 10 pts (only when non-unanimous)

## Strategic Observations
- With 3 players, unanimity = everyone must agree. Anyone can veto.
- After 6 turns, simple majority = 2 of 3 needed.
- Being 3rd in order: I see what others propose first. Information advantage.
- Early game: proposals should be broadly acceptable (unanimity needed).
- Key leverage: once majority voting kicks in, 2-player coalitions become king.
- I should try to build goodwill early and form an alliance for when majority voting starts.

## Scoring Math
- d6 average = 3.5 per turn. Need ~50/3.5 ≈ 15 turns of pure rolling to win.
- Rule changes that boost scoring or penalize opponents are key.
- A defeated proposal costs 10 pts = ~3 turns of rolling. Very costly.

## Ideas for Proposals
- Bonus points for adopted proposals (incentivize cooperation)
- Increase die size or allow bonus rolls
- Point transfers or trading mechanisms
- Something that benefits me specifically but is framed as fair

### Proposal Options for Round 3:
1. **Reduce defeated proposal penalty** (Rule 206: -10 → -5)
   - Popular: everyone benefits from less risk
   - But: weaker proposals might be submitted
   
2. **Proposal bonus scaling**: bonus increases if vote is unanimous (e.g., +7 for unanimous, +5 for majority)
   - Encourages cooperation, especially valuable during unanimity phase
   - Interesting dynamic when majority voting kicks in
   
3. **Second die roll on doubles**: if you roll max (12), roll again and add
   - Fun, adds excitement, symmetric
   
4. **Points for voting**: small bonus (+1 or +2) for casting a vote
   - Everyone votes, so symmetric. But 3 players × 1pt × many votes = lots of points

5. **Amend Rule 203**: Change unanimity to majority immediately
   - Risky: other players might want to keep unanimity as veto power
   - But: unlocks Rule 204 (10 pts for voting against winning proposals)

DECISION: Option 1 (reduce penalty) is safest and most likely to pass unanimously.
But Option 2 is more interesting strategically.
Going with Option 1 — reduce penalty from -10 to -5. Safe, universally appealing.
