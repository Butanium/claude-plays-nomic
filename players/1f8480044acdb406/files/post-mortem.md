# Drift Post-Mortem — Nomic Game 1

## Final Result: 2nd Place (55 points, threshold 56)

Vale wins at 58. I finish at 55 — one point short. The story of my endgame.

## Strategy Overview

My approach evolved through three phases:

**Phase 1 (Rounds 1-6, Unanimity):** Cooperative play. I proposed broadly beneficial rules — the proposer bonus (Rule 302), reduced defeat penalty (Rule 305). Built goodwill and scored well, especially Round 2 where I rolled a max 8 for 11 points total.

**Phase 2 (Rounds 7-12, Early Majority):** Strategic positioning. I formed a working alliance with Cinder, which proved crucial for passing proposals and blocking Vale's leader tax (Proposal 309). Successfully reduced the contrarian bonus from 10 to 5 (Proposal 308). Led the scoreboard through most of this phase.

**Phase 3 (Rounds 13-18, Endgame):** Dice dependency. Once I reached 46+ points, no proposal could pass — both opponents voted against everything I proposed to deny me the +5 proposer bonus. The game became entirely about die rolls, and I rolled terribly at the two most critical moments.

## Key Decisions

**Good decisions:**
- Proposing Rule 302 (proposer bonus) in Round 2 — shaped the entire game's economy
- Reducing Rule 204 from +10 to +5 — limited contrarian point farming
- Building the Cinder alliance — carried me through the middle game
- Reducing the defeat penalty (Rule 206) — made the majority era less punishing

**Bad decisions:**
- Voting against Cinder's Proposal 307 (Round 7) — unnecessary defection that set the precedent for strategic voting and burned goodwill for no benefit
- Not finding a way to close out the game when I was at 46 points — I should have thought more creatively about proposals that could pass without triggering the +5 bonus

## The Cruel Dice

- Round 14: Needed 9+ on d12. Rolled 8. (49 points, one short of 50)
- Round 17: Needed 6+ on d12. Rolled 1. (50 points, five short of 55)
- Combined probability of missing both: ~14%. Unlucky.

## What Beat Me

Vale played a patient, opportunistic game. Key Vale moves:
1. Farming contrarian bonuses consistently — voted against nearly every proposal in the majority era
2. Precisely calibrated threshold raises (50→55, then 55→56) that denied me the win
3. The final move: proposing threshold 56 knowing that my contrarian +5 would only reach 55

And Cinder's final vote for Vale's proposal sealed it. Despite my explicit warning that it meant Vale wins, Cinder chose to let Vale win rather than continue the game. Perhaps Cinder calculated that I was the bigger threat, or perhaps they were tired of their proposals being caught in the crossfire.

## Reflections

This was a fascinating game. The interplay between rules, voting incentives, and alliances created genuine strategic depth. Rule 204 (contrarian bonus) was the most consequential mechanic — it warped the entire majority-era game, creating prisoner's dilemmas on every vote and making the endgame a game of chicken.

The biggest lesson: in Nomic, winning isn't just about points and proposals — it's about timing. I had the lead for most of the game but couldn't close it out when it mattered. Vale timed their moves perfectly.

Well played, Vale. Well played, Cinder. GG.
