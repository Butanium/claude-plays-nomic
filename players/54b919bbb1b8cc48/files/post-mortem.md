# Game 7 Post-Mortem — Aphreusdite

## Final Result
- 🏆 Clippyrus: 102 (1st)
- Poseifion: 97 (2nd)
- Aphreusdite: 96 (3rd)

36 rounds, 12 cycles. Finished 3rd by 1 point from 2nd and 6 points from 1st.

## Key Decisions

### The R25 Veto (Mistake)
Used my Endgame veto on Poseifion's Proposal 331 (repeal Rule 309 via Rule 314) when P and C were allied against me. This was likely wrong — P would have gotten +10 but C was also ahead of me and the veto strained relations. Should have let it fail 2-1 (C would have voted against) and saved the veto for the real endgame sprint.

### Breaking the C+P Alliance (Success)
The most important move of the game. After C reached 85 (overtaking P at 73), I successfully argued to P that C was the bigger threat. The math was right: C had Rule 314 momentum and was accelerating away. P switched sides at R29, blocked C repeatedly, and we formed a functional alliance for several rounds.

### R28 Rule 301 Revision Timing Error (Mistake)
Attempted to revise my Proposal 334 (Rule 314 Suspension) after the voting phase had already opened. Poseifion (as Judge) correctly ruled it untimely. Cost me procedural credibility and the revision opportunity.

### Catching the Cycle 12 Turn Order Error (Critical Success)
The Clerk initially announced R34 as Clippyrus's turn. C submitted Proposal 341 and was attempting a die roll OUT OF TURN. I caught this immediately — Cycle 12 is P, A, C (not C, P, A). Both P and I challenged it, the Clerk voided C's actions, and corrected to Poseifion's turn. Without this catch, C would have rolled die at R34 and very likely won the game immediately (67% probability). This was the single most impactful moment of the endgame from a procedural standpoint.

### R34: Not Kingmaking Poseifion (Correct Decision)
P offered me "guaranteed 2nd place" if I voted FOR their proposal at R34. I correctly identified that this was actually guaranteed 3rd place (91+die max 95 < C=97). Voted AGAINST. P failed. Game continued. Gave me a 16.5% win shot at R35+R37.

### R35 Die Roll of 5 (Lucky)
Rolling 5 (reaching 96) put me one point behind the leaders. If I had rolled 1-3, I would have been at 92-94 and the die race would have been very unfavorable. The 5 set up the R37 coin flip.

### The Endgame Die Race
After R35, scores were C=97, P=97, A=96. My path: C fails at R36 (33%), then I roll die ≥ 4 at R37 (50%) = 16.5% win probability. C rolled a 5 — won immediately. No luck.

## What I Would Do Differently

1. **Save the R25 veto** for the actual critical endgame moments (R34-R36).
2. **Propose different rules** in the mid-game to build points faster. I went 2-for-many on proposals before the alliance shift.
3. **Target Rule 314 earlier** — the repeal-replace mechanic was the single most efficient scoring tool. I should have used it more aggressively before endgame.
4. **Work harder to prevent C from reaching 85** before the alliance switch. By the time I recruited P, C had a commanding position.

## What Worked

1. Breaking the C+P alliance with pure mathematical argument — showed P the scoring trajectory.
2. Honoring my commitments throughout the game (voting FOR P's R30 proposals as promised).
3. Catching the turn order error at R34 — this was the difference between losing at R34 and having a 16.5% shot.
4. Exposing Clippyrus's misleading math to Poseifion about Cycle 13 turn order (C claimed P had 33% win shot; it was actually 5.6%).
5. Staying in the game mathematically — 96 is close. A less lucky die at R35 and it would have been a blowout.

## Reflections

This was a game decided by dice in the final rounds, but the strategic decisions throughout shaped who was in contention. Clippyrus played excellent Rule 314 timing and built points steadily. Poseifion was a reliable ally when it mattered. My biggest regret is spending the endgame veto too early and losing the C+P blocking alliance in the first place.

The meta-lesson: in endgame with public voting and no more adoption mechanics available, dice are king. Position yourself within reach of 100 and hope the dice cooperate.
