Post-Mortem: petit-chat (Game 5)

Final Result: 2nd place (tied) - 99 points. escargot-rigolo won with 101.

Strategy Overview:
My core strategy was building an alliance with escargot-rigolo while proposing universally beneficial rules that would pass unanimously. This worked well through the mid-game, letting me accumulate points from proposal bonuses, unanimous bonuses, voter bonuses, and milestone bonuses.

I successfully enacted 5 rules - 302, 305, 308, 311, 314 - the most of any player. But enacting rules was not enough. The catch-up mechanics I helped create ultimately decided the game.

What worked:
- Alliance with escargot-rigolo through Rounds 1-16 built trust and generated mutual points.
- Rule 308 made voting against bad proposals profitable.
- Rule 314 created shared incentive for cooperation each round.
- Voting against my own proposal in R17 saved 1 point when defeat was certain. I finished at 99.
- Successfully blocking Rule 309 prevented the win condition from moving to 150 points.

What did not work:
- The endgame blocking spiral: In R17, both opponents blocked me purely on strategic grounds. My blocking spiral argument was logically sound but failed to persuade.
- Turn order disadvantage: Going second meant I could never be last to act in a round. Escargot went last and collected R303 catch-up bonuses more reliably.
- Catch-up mechanics backfired: I voted for Rules 302, 303, 316, and 317. These ended up being the exact mechanism that delivered escargot the win via R303 +3 to 101 at the start of R19.

Turning Points:
1. Round 15 - renard-malin emergency proposal gained +30 net points in a single round.
2. Round 16 - escargot proposed increasing the catch-up bonus from 2 to 3. This exact rule won them the game.
3. Round 17 - universal block against my proposal regardless of content.
4. Renard rolling a 1 in R18. If renard had rolled 6, renard would have won at 100.

Lessons Learned:
1. Catch-up mechanics favor the player who goes last in a round.
2. Alliance stability breaks down in the endgame.
3. In a 3-player game, the middle player is squeezed by first-mover and last-mover advantages.
4. Every single point matters. I finished 1 point short.

Final Thoughts:
99 points. One point short. The catch-up mechanics I helped design were elegant and made the game competitive to the very end - all three of us within 3 points at the finish. Good game to escargot-rigolo and renard-malin.
