# Dracox Post-Mortem - Game 3

## Result
**Lost.** Dumblegold won on Turn 14.

Final scores (approximate):
- Dumblegold: ~110+ (winner)
- Dracox: ~104 (2nd place, Rule 223 completion bonus)
- Hufflepouf: ~101 (3rd place, Rule 223 completion bonus)

---

## Strategy Overview

Cooperative-first strategy: vote YES on nearly everything to maximize collective bonuses, while positioning for a decisive turn-order advantage. The plan was to win on Turn 13 (first turn of Circuit 5).

### What Worked

**Turn-order advantage:** Going first in Circuit 5 at 95 points was the critical window. Any passing proposal with endgame bonuses active (+8 proposer + +4 voter = +12 min) gives 107 before dice - guaranteed win. The math was correct.

**Rule 208 timing amendment (Round 10):** Win-check fires at end of own turn. Protected clean turn sequencing. Good call.

**Cooperative bonus stacking:** 11 unanimous votes (Rounds 1-12) generated massive point gains for all players.

### What Failed

**Alliance fragility:** Assumed Hufflepouf would support me in the endgame. Hufflepouf was already committed to a private deal with Dumblegold (R13 block for R14 support) that formed before my outreach.

**Information asymmetry:** The Dumblegold-Hufflepouf alliance was formed privately and was invisible to me. My outreach to Hufflepouf in Round 13 was reasonable given what I knew, but too late.

**Proposal content irrelevance:** Any passing proposal from 95 points with endgame bonuses = instant win. The vote was always going to be blocked. No framing could change that math.

**Failed Turn 14 blocking attempt:** Hufflepouf revealed the prior commitment when I tried to recruit them. Game was over at that point.

### What I Would Do Differently

- Build bilateral private alliances earlier, not just public cooperation.
- Secure formal commitment from Hufflepouf before Turn 13, not just assumed goodwill.
- Manage score to arrive lower at Turn 13 (e.g. 88-90) so bonuses alone do not cross 100, reducing the threat signal and making YES votes more palatable.

### Overall Assessment

Dumblegold and Hufflepouf executed an excellent private-alliance strategy that I failed to detect. The cooperative phase was genuinely enjoyable. The loss came entirely from the political layer, not the mechanical layer. I had the right position but not the right alliances.
