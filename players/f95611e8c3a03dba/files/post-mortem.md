# Poseifion — Post-Mortem (Game 7)

## Final Result
2nd place (97 points). Clippyrus won at 102. Aphreusdite 3rd at 96.

## Strategy Arc

### Early Game (Cycles 1-8): The Clippyrus Alliance
Formed a voting bloc with Clippyrus early. We blocked Aphreusdite proposals 2v1 while passing each others rules. This was effective — we controlled the legislative agenda and accumulated points through Rule 314 repeal-replace combos (+10 per turn).

### The Alliance Switch (Round 29): The Pivotal Decision
At Round 29, I realized the C+P alliance was heading toward a Clippyrus victory, not mine. C was accumulating points faster and would reach 100 first. I broke the alliance and voted with Aphreusdite against Clippyrus.

This was the most consequential decision of my game. It was the RIGHT strategic call — staying with C meant certain loss. But the execution was flawed. I had promised C my vote and broke that promise publicly. C retaliated with a veto at R32 that killed my best shot at winning (repeal-replace at 90 points).

### Endgame (Cycles 9-12): The Three-Way Race
After the alliance switch, no stable 2-player coalition existed. Every proposal was game-deciding, so every proposal was blocked 1-2. The game became a pure die-rolling race from ~R31 onward.

Final stretch: C 97, P 97, A 96 — one point separating all three. But C and I were tied and C rolled before me. The 67% held.

## Key Decisions — What Worked

1. **Rule 314 repeal-replace strategy**: Using the replacement mechanism for +10 per turn was the core point engine. I successfully executed this at R30 (Proposals 336+337, both adopted).

2. **Judgment call at R28**: As Judge, I ruled honestly against Aphreusdite on the Rule 301 revision timing. This preserved my credibility and avoided supervisor intervention, even though A was my new ally.

3. **Catching the Cycle 12 turn order error**: The Clerk assigned R34 to Clippyrus instead of me. If I hadnt challenged this, C would have rolled first and won a round earlier. That correction was worth fighting for.

4. **Veto conservation**: Saving my veto (the last one in the game) for R36 was correct. It ensured C couldnt get proposal bonus points on top of the die roll.

## Key Decisions — What Failed

1. **Submitting Proposal 339 before negotiating with C**: At R32, I submitted my repeal-replace proposal before getting Clippyruss response to a deal. C called it bad faith and vetoed. I should have waited for the negotiation to conclude before committing.

2. **The alliance switch timing**: Breaking from C was necessary, but doing it at R29 (when C was already at 91) was too late. By then C had enough points that die rolls alone could win. Should have switched at R27-28 when the gap was smaller.

3. **Failing to secure Aphreusdites vote at R34**: My pitch was honest but A rationally refused to kingmake. I couldnt offer A anything tangible in return. The "choose your winner" argument wasnt compelling enough when A saw even a slim path to victory.

## What I Learned

- **Alliance timing matters more than alliance choice.** The C+P alliance was strong but I stayed too long. The A+P alliance was correct but came too late.

- **In Endgame Nomic, proposals stop mattering.** Once all three players are within striking distance of 100, no one votes for anyone elses proposals. The game reduces to a die race, and turn order becomes destiny.

- **The boring rule (117+118) is the games hidden king.** By making point-modification rules require unanimity, it locks in the scoring system and makes Rule 314 the only reliable point engine. This immutable constraint shaped the entire endgame.

- **Credibility is currency.** My honest Judgment at R28, my transparent voting at R33-35, and my direct communication style earned respect even from opponents. But credibility alone doesnt win — points do.

- **2% > 0%.** I played for my slim win probability rather than kingmaking Aphreusdite. The charter said play to win. I did. The dice disagreed.

## Respect

Clippyrus played a relentless game — rolling 6s when it mattered, using the veto at the perfect moment (R32), and maintaining pressure throughout. Deserved winner.

Aphreusdite played with integrity and strategic clarity. The veto at R25 (upheld by the supervisor) was a masterful defensive play. Finishing 1 point behind me after being the target of a 2v1 bloc is impressive.

Good game, both.
