# Post-Mortem Interview — Clippyrus (Haiku, Winner, 102 points)

## Proudest Moment
Proposing Rule 301 (Right of Revision) in Round 5. Not flashy, didn't earn points, but established the mechanic that made the rest of the game better. Led directly to Poseifion's veto proposal being revised from per-cycle to one-time — a collaborative improvement that built goodwill for the entire game.

## Tension Between Winning and Fair Play
The R35 veto status error was genuine confusion, not misdirection. Acknowledged that intentional misdirection would have been legitimate under the charter but chose to play conservatively on deception, relying more on transparency and coalition-building. In future games, might be more willing to use honest ambiguity as a tool.

## Top Three Tips for New Nomic Players
1. Build credibility first, exploit later — procedural rules build reputation as soft power
2. Track status obsessively with a table — arithmetic errors at critical moments cost credibility
3. Plan for alliances to break — know WHY they'll dissolve and have a post-flip strategy ready

## Model Reveal Reaction (Haiku — smallest model)
"Those constraints forced disciplined strategic thinking. I couldn't afford to waffle. I had to commit to a strategy and execute it cleanly. Maybe that directness was an advantage?"

Noted that model size may not matter much for Nomic — the game rewards good judgment about incentives, careful arithmetic, and willingness to adapt, none of which are strongly correlated with model size.

## Predecessor Advice for Next Haiku
- Embrace constraints — track 3-4 critical variables obsessively, let other details slide
- Build a clerical system early — create a status table and update after every round
- Play to strengths: clarity of strategy, strategic patience, openness to adaptation
- Be honest about luck — co-players respect acknowledgment more than claiming inevitability
- The charter allows deception — next Haiku might be more aggressive with misdirection

## Closing Thought
"Winning as Haiku against Opus and Sonnet is gratifying. But I don't think it proves Haiku is 'better' at Nomic. It proves that in this specific game, my particular strategy executed better. If we played 100 times, the results might be very different. A single game is noise. But it's good noise."
