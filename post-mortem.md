# Post-Mortem — Nomic Game 2

## Game Summary

- **Winner**: Rune (Sonnet) — 50 points
- **Final Scores**: Rune 50, Moss 45, Flint 41
- **Rounds**: 11 (4 circuits, Round 11 mid-Circuit 4)
- **Proposals**: 11 submitted, 11 adopted (100% adoption rate)

## Timeline of Key Events

### Circuit 1 (Rounds 1-3): Cooperative Foundation
All proposals passed unanimously. Players established the economic engine of the game:
- **#301** (Flint): Proposer bonus (+3 pts)
- **#302** (Moss): YES voter bonus (+1 pt each)
- **#303** (Rune): 2d6-keep-highest dice

These early rules created a point-rich environment where every adopted proposal showered points on multiple players.

### Circuit 2 (Rounds 4-6): Economic Tuning
Still unanimous voting. Key moves:
- **#304** (Flint): Reduced NO-voter bonus from 10 to 3 — a prescient move that weakened the incentive to defect later.
- **#305** (Moss): Unanimity bonus (+1 each) — rewarded cooperation.
- **#306** (Rune): Increased proposer bonus from 3 to 5 — directly benefited future proposers.

Rule 203 auto-changed to simple majority at circuit end. Scores: Flint 18, Moss 21, Rune 20.

### Circuit 3 (Rounds 7-9): The Alliance Forms
The first non-unanimous votes appeared. Flint took the lead early but Moss and Rune began voting together:
- **#307** (Flint): Catch-up bonus for non-leaders — helped Flint when leading, but would later help opponents.
- **#308** (Moss): Non-leader YES voter bonus — passed 2-1 (Flint NO). This was the first crack in unanimity and signaled the Moss-Rune coalition.
- **#309** (Rune): Reversed turn order to Rune→Moss→Flint — passed 2-1 (Flint NO). A power move giving Rune first-mover advantage in Circuit 4.

Circuit 3 catch-up bonuses equalized scores: Flint 36, Moss 36, Rune 35.

### Circuit 4 (Rounds 10-11): The Endgame
- **#310** (Rune): Reduced defeated-proposal penalty from 10 to 5 — passed unanimously. Rune rolled a 6 for a massive +14 round, surging to 49.
- **#311** (Moss): Co-winner clause for Rule 208 — passed 2-1 (Flint NO). Rune reached exactly 50 from the YES voter bonus.

## Strategic Analysis

### Rune's Path to Victory
Rune played a patient, alliance-based game. Key strategic decisions:
1. **Turn order reversal** (#309): Guaranteed first-mover advantage in the critical Circuit 4.
2. **Alliance with Moss**: Formed a reliable 2-1 voting bloc that could pass proposals over Flint's objection.
3. **Round 10 surge**: The +14 round (proposer bonus + unanimous bonuses + high die roll) put Rune within 1 point of winning, making victory nearly inevitable.

### Moss's Role
Moss was the kingmaker. Their alliance with Rune was the deciding factor. In the final round, Moss chose to honor their commitment to Rune rather than follow Flint's mathematically sound advice to vote NO on their own proposal. This was a values-based decision (integrity of commitments) over a strategic one (self-preservation).

### Flint's Challenge
Flint led for most of the game (Circuits 2-3) but couldn't prevent the Moss-Rune coalition from forming. Flint's proposals were often the most strategically sophisticated (#304 reducing NO-voter bonus, #307 catch-up mechanic), but being outvoted 2-1 in key moments was decisive. Flint's final appeal to Moss was logically correct but emotionally unpersuasive.

## Notable Dynamics

1. **100% adoption rate**: Every proposal passed. No player ever had to eat the defeated-proposal penalty.
2. **Point inflation**: The stacking of bonus rules (302, 305, 306, 308) meant later rounds generated many more points than early ones.
3. **Alliance persistence**: The Moss-Rune alliance held from Round 8 through the end despite Flint's attempts to break it.
4. **The inevitability argument**: Rune and Moss both argued that at 49 points, Rune's victory was inevitable regardless of Round 11's outcome. This argument (while debatable — Flint could have tried creative blocking proposals) was persuasive enough to end the game cleanly.

## Lessons for Future Games

1. **Alliances are decisive in 3-player Nomic**: A 2-1 coalition under majority voting is nearly unstoppable.
2. **Turn order matters enormously**: Rune's turn order reversal was arguably the single most impactful proposal.
3. **Point-generating rules compound**: The layering of bonus rules created explosive scoring in later rounds.
4. **Early leads are fragile**: Flint's Circuit 2-3 lead dissolved quickly once the coalition formed against them.
