# Post-Mortem: Nomic Game 5

## Winner
**escargot-rigolo (Haiku)** — 101 points, winning via Rule 303 catch-up bonus at the start of Round 19.

## Final Scores
- escargot-rigolo: **101**
- petit-chat: 99
- renard-malin: 99

The closest possible finish — all three players within 2 points.

## Game Arc

### Early Game (Rounds 1-6): The Cooperative Phase
All three players focused on building a shared bonus ecosystem. Rules 301 (unanimous bonus), 302 (catch-up), 303 (turn-start catch-up), and 305 (voter bonus) were all adopted unanimously. The only dissent was Proposal 304 (second-place bonus), which was rejected — establishing an important principle that "too many position-based bonuses distort the game."

### Mid Game (Rounds 7-12): Ecosystem Building
Players continued cooperating, adding Rules 307 (emergency proposals), 308 (against-vote bonus), 310 (milestone bonuses), 311 (valid vote bonus), and 312 (reduced defeat penalty). Proposal 309 (raise win condition to 150) was the first truly contentious vote — renard-malin proposed it, escargot-rigolo initially supported it but then dramatically flipped after petit-chat's persuasive counter-argument about precedent-setting. This was a pivotal moment in the game's social dynamics.

### Late Game (Rounds 13-15): renard-malin's Surge
renard-malin made a brilliant strategic play in Round 15, invoking the emergency proposal mechanic (Rule 307) to submit TWO proposals in one turn. Both passed unanimously, triggering two milestone bonuses. renard-malin surged from 55 to 90 in a single round — the most dramatic scoring swing of the game.

### Endgame (Rounds 16-18): The Blocking Spiral
With scores at 82/81/90, the game entered a blocking phase. renard-malin publicly declared they would vote against any petit-chat proposal in Round 17 (since any adopted proposal could hand petit-chat the win). escargot-rigolo joined the coalition. petit-chat responded by voting against their own Proposal 318 — collecting the Rule 308 "against" bonus while minimizing losses. In Round 18, renard-malin's proposal was similarly blocked. The endgame became a pure dice-roll race with voting bonuses providing the marginal points.

### The Winning Move
The irony: escargot-rigolo won via the Rule 303 catch-up bonus. At 98 points (the lowest score by 1 point), escargot-rigolo received +3 at the start of Round 19, reaching 101 without even needing to propose or roll. The mechanic designed to help trailing players delivered the final blow.

## Key Turning Points

1. **Round 9 — The Flip:** escargot-rigolo initially supported renard-malin's proposal to raise the win condition from 100 to 150, then dramatically changed their vote after petit-chat's persuasive counter-argument. This established petit-chat as the most persuasive debater and showed escargot-rigolo's willingness to change position based on arguments.

2. **Round 15 — The Emergency Proposal:** renard-malin's use of Rule 307 to submit two proposals in one turn, triggering two milestone bonuses, was the game's most brilliant tactical move. It vaulted them from last place to first.

3. **Rounds 17-18 — The Blocking Coalition:** The endgame blocking spiral was a textbook game theory moment. When any adopted proposal could win the game for the proposer, the rational move for opponents was always to block. petit-chat's innovation of voting against their own proposal was a clever counter-strategy.

4. **The Catch-Up Win:** escargot-rigolo's victory via Rule 303 was deeply ironic — they benefited from being 1 point behind. The catch-up mechanics that all three players cooperatively built ultimately decided the winner.

## Rules Evolution
The game produced a rich scoring ecosystem:
- **Proposer rewards:** +7 base (amended from 5) + 2 unanimous bonus
- **Voter rewards:** +2 for "for" on adopted, +1 for "against" on defeated, +1 per valid vote
- **Position-based:** +3 turn-start catch-up, +4 end-of-round catch-up
- **Milestones:** +5 for 2nd/3rd/4th enacted rules
- **Round cooperation:** +1 per adopted proposal per round
- **Emergency proposals:** 6-point cost for extra proposal (once per game)
- **Defeat penalty:** 3 points (reduced from 5)

## Lessons Learned
1. Catch-up mechanics are powerful — they literally won the game.
2. The blocking spiral in endgame is a natural but challenging dynamic.
3. The emergency proposal mechanic (Rule 307) created the game's most dramatic moment.
4. Voting against your own proposal is a valid endgame strategy when defeat is inevitable.
5. The cooperative phase built a rich ecosystem, but the endgame showed the limits of cooperation when winning is at stake.
